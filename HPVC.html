Our Projects

Human Power Vehicle Contest(HPVC)



The Human Powered Vehicle (HPV) team provides an opportunity for students to demonstrate the application of sound engineering design principles in the development of sustainable and practical transportation alternatives. In the HPVC, students work in teams to design and build efficient, highly engineered vehicles for everyday useâ€”from commuting to work, to carrying goods to market.





The Team builds a bicycle-based aerodynamically faired vehicle every year, and competes in a competition put on by the American Society of Mechanical Engineers (ASME). The competition is part design event, part innovation, part sprint race, and part endurance race. Team ASME IITR participated in ASME E-Fest Asia Pacific held in Jaipur.



  1. We grabbed 11th and 12th position out of 42 teams in design and innovation respectively in Human Powered Vehicle Challenge(HPVC) in our very first year of participation.
  2. Team member Vibhu Vaibhav secured 1st position in Old Gaurd Oral Presentation grabbing prize money of Rs 50,000 and gets a chance to represent Asia in Florida, USA for the same at international level.
  3. We also marked our performance in an IAM3D challenge as one of the top 3 and the team will be mentored by ASME personnel for competing at international level.
